The Official Final Result

Spain defeated Argentina 1-0 after extra time in the FIFA World Cup 2026 final at New York New Jersey Stadium on 19 July. FIFA's official match report records Ferran Torres as the scorer of the decisive goal in the 106th minute.

The win made Spain world champions for the second time. Argentina entered the match as reigning champions, while Spain arrived as European champions, giving the final a clear clash of recent football powers.

Why One Goal Carried So Much Weight

A scoreless first 90 minutes made every late decision feel larger. Goalkeeping, defensive concentration and the ability to stay patient mattered as much as attacking flair.

Torres entered as a substitute and finished the move that separated the teams. For fans, it was a reminder that a final can turn on readiness: a player may spend most of the match watching before one moment demands total commitment.

Africa's Place in the 48-Team Story

The first 48-team men's World Cup featured ten teams from the CAF qualified list: Algeria, Cabo Verde, Congo DR, Côte d'Ivoire, Egypt, Ghana, Morocco, Senegal, South Africa and Tunisia.

That wider presence gave supporters more regional stories to follow before the final. Cabo Verde's debut and the mix of returning teams showed why format changes matter beyond the champion alone.
