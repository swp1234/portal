How to Play Maze Runner

Maze Runner is easy to pick up but offers layers of depth that reveal themselves as you progress through increasingly complex levels. Here is everything you need to know to get started.

Basic Controls

  • Desktop: Use arrow keys or WASD to move your character through the corridors.
  • Mobile: Swipe in any direction to move. Touch controls respond to quick flicks for fast movement or held swipes for continuous motion.
  • Collect all keys scattered through the maze to unlock the exit gate.
  • Grab bonus stars along the way for extra points and combo multipliers.
  • Reach the exit to complete the level and advance to the next maze.
Pro Tip: You do not need to collect every star to complete a level — only the keys are mandatory. However, stars build your combo multiplier, so collecting them along efficient routes dramatically increases your score.

Game Modes Explained

Each of Maze Runner's three modes transforms the core maze-solving experience into something fundamentally different. Mastering all three requires distinct skill sets and strategies.

Normal Mode

The classic maze experience with full visibility of the entire layout. You can see every wall, every key, and the exit from the start. No timer pressure means you can plan your route carefully before making a single move. Ideal for beginners learning maze-solving fundamentals, and for experienced players who enjoy optimizing their path for maximum star collection.

Fog of War Mode

Your vision is limited to a small radius around your character. The rest of the maze is shrouded in darkness. You must explore, memorize, and mentally map the layout as you move. Dead ends are invisible until you walk into them. This mode tests spatial memory and pattern recognition more than any other, and it transforms a familiar maze into an entirely different challenge.

Time Attack Mode

A countdown timer ticks down from the moment the maze appears. You must collect all keys and reach the exit before time expires. Every second counts, and hesitation at intersections can be the difference between success and failure. Time bonuses appear throughout the maze, and the Time Freeze power-up becomes your most valuable asset.

Keys and Collectibles

Every maze contains items that affect your score, progression, and abilities. Understanding what to prioritize is essential for high-level play.

  • Keys: Mandatory collectibles. You must find every key in the maze before the exit gate unlocks. Keys are often placed in remote corners or behind tricky paths to force exploration.
  • Bonus Stars: Optional collectibles that award points and build your combo multiplier. Collecting stars in quick succession without stopping increases the multiplier, leading to exponentially higher scores.
  • Power-ups: Glowing icons that grant temporary abilities. They appear at random locations and disappear after a set time if not collected.
Combo System: Each star collected within a few seconds of the previous one extends your combo chain. A 5-star combo awards significantly more points than five individual stars collected minutes apart. Plan your routes to chain stars together for maximum scoring potential.

Strategy by Mode

Normal Mode Strategy

With full visibility and no time pressure, Normal Mode is about optimization. Before moving, take a few seconds to scan the entire maze. Identify where all keys are located. Plan a single efficient route that collects every key and as many stars as possible without excessive backtracking. The best Normal Mode players rarely retrace their steps — every move is purposeful.

Route Planning Technique

Start by locating the exit and all keys. Then mentally trace a path that connects them in a logical sequence, minimizing the total distance traveled. Think of it like solving the "traveling salesman problem" — you want the shortest possible route that visits every required point. Prioritize keys in dead-end branches first, then sweep back toward the exit.

Fog of War Strategy

Limited visibility makes this mode a true test of memory and orientation. The wall-following technique is your most reliable tool: choose either the left or right wall at the start and follow it consistently. This guarantees you will eventually traverse the entire maze, discovering every key and path. While not the fastest method, it is the safest.

Complement wall-following with mental mapping. As you explore, build a mental picture of the maze layout. Note where dead ends are so you never waste time entering them twice. When you spot a Vision Expander power-up, grab it immediately — those extra seconds of expanded sight let you update your mental map significantly.

Time Attack Strategy

Every second is precious in Time Attack. Speed and decisiveness beat caution. Use the split-second decision rule: at every intersection, commit to a direction within one second. Hesitation adds up fast. If you hit a dead end, turn around immediately without pausing to think.

Prioritize Time Freeze power-ups above everything else. Five seconds of frozen time can be the difference between completing the maze and running out of time. Combine Time Freeze with Speed Boost for explosive bursts of progress through the longest corridors.

Advanced Tips

  1. Learn to read maze patterns. Procedural generation follows rules. After enough games, you will start recognizing common corridor shapes, junction types, and dead-end indicators. This pattern recognition dramatically speeds up navigation.
  2. Dead ends have a tell. In most procedurally generated mazes, corridors that narrow to a single-width path leading away from the center are more likely to be dead ends. Learn to spot these and avoid them unless they contain a key.
  3. Use the edges. Maze perimeters are predictable — they always end at a wall. Following a perimeter first can reveal the overall maze dimensions and locate keys placed in corner positions.
  4. Chain stars near keys. Since you must visit key locations anyway, plan your star collection around those paths. Grab stars on the approach to each key to maintain your combo multiplier.
  5. Practice Fog of War to improve in all modes. The spatial memory skills you develop in Fog of War transfer directly to faster decision-making in Normal and Time Attack modes.

Why Maze Games Train Your Brain

Maze navigation is more than entertainment — it is a genuine cognitive workout. Research in spatial cognition has shown that maze-solving activates the hippocampus, the brain region responsible for spatial memory and navigation. Regular maze practice strengthens neural pathways associated with spatial reasoning, working memory, and problem-solving.

Spatial awareness improves as you learn to build and maintain mental maps. This skill transfers to real-world navigation, reading floor plans, and understanding complex spatial relationships.

Decision-making speed increases through the constant practice of evaluating options at intersections. Time Attack mode specifically trains rapid assessment under pressure, a skill valuable in countless real-life scenarios.

Working memory gets a serious workout in Fog of War mode. Holding a mental map of explored areas while simultaneously navigating unexplored regions pushes your working memory capacity to its limits and gradually expands it.

Unlike passive brain games, maze navigation requires active engagement of multiple cognitive systems simultaneously: visual processing, motor planning, spatial memory, and executive decision-making. This multi-system activation is what makes maze games particularly effective as cognitive training.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you play Maze Runner?

Use arrow keys or swipe gestures to navigate through procedurally generated mazes. Collect all keys to unlock the exit gate, grab bonus stars for extra points and combo multipliers, and reach the exit to advance. Choose from three modes: Normal (full visibility, no timer), Fog of War (limited vision), or Time Attack (countdown timer).

What are the three game modes?

Normal Mode offers full maze visibility with no time pressure, ideal for planning optimal routes. Fog of War Mode limits your vision to a small radius, requiring spatial memory and careful exploration. Time Attack Mode adds a countdown timer, demanding speed and efficient pathfinding to reach the exit before time expires.

How do power-ups work in Maze Runner?

Power-ups appear as glowing icons throughout the maze. Speed Boost doubles your movement speed, Time Freeze pauses the countdown in Time Attack mode, Vision Expander increases your visible area in Fog of War, Key Magnet attracts nearby keys, and Score Multiplier doubles your points. Each lasts a limited duration and they cannot be stacked.

What is the best strategy for Fog of War mode?

Use the wall-following technique: pick one wall (left or right) and follow it consistently to guarantee full maze coverage. Build a mental map of explored areas, note dead ends to avoid revisiting them, and prioritize Vision Expander power-ups. Move deliberately rather than rushing into unseen corridors.

Are the mazes randomly generated?

Yes, every maze is procedurally generated, ensuring no two playthroughs are identical. The algorithm guarantees a valid path from start to exit, reachable keys, and difficulty that scales with your level. Maze size and complexity increase progressively, starting from simple grids and expanding to large, intricate layouts.
