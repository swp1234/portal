The brick breaker genre is one of the most enduring in gaming history. Taito's 1986 Arkanoid elevated the formula with power-ups and varied brick types, cementing it as a permanent fixture in arcade culture. Today, the ball-and-paddle mechanic remains as satisfying as ever.

How to Play Brick Breaker

The objective is straightforward: use your paddle to bounce a ball into the brick formation above, destroying every breakable brick to advance to the next stage. The challenge comes from controlling the ball's trajectory, managing your three lives, and reacting to the increasing complexity of brick layouts across 20 stages.

Controls

  • Mouse: Move the cursor left and right to position the paddle. The paddle follows your mouse with smooth interpolation.
  • Touch: Drag your finger horizontally across the screen. The paddle tracks your touch position.
  • Keyboard: Use the left and right arrow keys to move the paddle. Press spacebar to pause or resume.
  • Launch: Click or tap anywhere on the game canvas to release the ball from the paddle.
  • You start with 3 lives. A life is lost each time the ball falls below the paddle. The game ends when all lives are gone.
  • Clear all breakable bricks on a stage to advance to the next one.
Pro tip: Where you hit the ball on the paddle matters. Hitting near the paddle's edge sends the ball at a sharp angle, while a center hit goes nearly straight up. Use edge hits to aim for specific sections of the brick wall.

Power-Ups Guide

When you destroy a brick, there is a 15% chance it drops a power-up. Each power-up collected also awards 50 bonus points. Learning to prioritize which power-ups to catch — and which situations benefit from each — is a key skill for reaching the higher stages.

Multi-Ball is the most impactful power-up in the game. With two or more balls in play, you clear bricks at double the rate and have a buffer — losing one ball does not cost a life as long as at least one ball remains. The downside is that tracking multiple balls demands more focus, and losing all of them at once can happen quickly if they cluster near the bottom.

Paddle Expand is the most reliable defensive power-up. A wider paddle covers more of the screen, reducing the chance that a ball slips past on an unexpected angle. It pairs well with Multi-Ball since the wider surface catches stray balls more easily.

Level Progression

Brick Breaker's 20 stages are divided into four difficulty tiers, each introducing new brick types and more complex patterns. Understanding what each tier demands helps you prepare mentally and adjust your strategy in advance.

Stages 1 – 4: Foundation

All bricks are Normal (one hit to destroy, red color). These stages exist to help you calibrate your paddle control and learn how the ball bounces off walls, the ceiling, and different paddle positions. Use this time to practice aiming for corners and edges of the formation, which is a skill you will need later. Scoring is straightforward: 10 points per brick destroyed.

Stages 5 – 7: Escalation

Stage 5 introduces Strong bricks (two hits to destroy, darker red). These bricks appear sporadically at first, mixed with normal bricks, then increase in density. By Stage 7, the entire formation consists of strong bricks, doubling the number of hits required to clear the board. Strong bricks award 20 points when destroyed (10 points per hit, doubled for two-hit health). Ball control becomes critical here because wasted bounces cost time and increase the risk of losing the ball.

Stages 8 – 10: Advanced

Two new brick types appear. Special bricks (three hits, gold color) require sustained targeting, and Unbreakable bricks (steel, dark gray) cannot be destroyed at all. Unbreakable bricks act as obstacles, redirecting the ball and protecting the bricks behind them. You must clear all breakable bricks while navigating around the steel walls. Special bricks award 30 points when destroyed. Stage 10 features unbreakable bricks lining both sides of the top row, creating a channel that forces the ball into the center.

Stages 11 – 20: Expert

These stages combine all four brick types in dense, complex patterns with unbreakable bricks forming walls and corridors. The game cycles its hardest pattern template, meaning every stage from 11 onward is a genuine test of skill. Expect formations where special and strong bricks are shielded behind rows of unbreakable steel. Reaching stage 15 is an achievement. Completing all 20 stages makes you a true Brick Breaker master.

Scoring System

Understanding how points accumulate helps you set meaningful goals and track your improvement over time.

  • Normal brick destroyed: 10 points
  • Strong brick destroyed: 20 points (10 per hit level)
  • Special brick destroyed: 30 points (10 per hit level)
  • Power-up collected: 50 points
  • Unbreakable bricks: 0 points (cannot be destroyed)

A perfect run through the early stages (32 normal bricks per stage, 4 stages) yields roughly 1,280 points before power-ups. Mid-game stages with strong bricks can yield 640+ points per stage. With power-up bonuses factored in, a solid run through 10 stages typically produces a score between 5,000 and 8,000. Scores above 10,000 indicate you have pushed well into the expert stages.

Strategies for Each Stage Tier

Early Stages (1 – 4): Build Momentum

  1. Aim for the sides. Try to slip the ball up through a gap at the edge of the brick formation. Once the ball is above the bricks, it ricochets between the ceiling and the top row, clearing whole sections without any paddle input.
  2. Catch every power-up. Power-ups on early stages are free value. The risks are low and any advantage carries forward into harder stages.
  3. Stay centered. Keep the paddle near the center of the screen. From the center, you can reach balls bouncing to either side with a shorter movement distance.

Mid Stages (5 – 7): Patience Pays

  1. Target strong bricks early. Strong bricks require two hits. If you leave them until last, you spend extra time bouncing the ball into scattered single bricks, which is harder to control.
  2. Prioritize Paddle Expand and Multi-Ball. The wider paddle gives you a safety margin, and multi-ball doubles your clearing speed against the tougher formations.
  3. Control your angles. Use deliberate edge hits on the paddle to steer the ball toward clusters of strong bricks rather than bouncing aimlessly.

Advanced Stages (8 – 10): Navigate the Steel

  1. Map the unbreakable bricks mentally. Before launching, study where the steel bricks sit. They redirect the ball in predictable ways — use them as backboards to reach bricks behind them.
  2. Save your laser for special bricks. If you collect a laser power-up, aim it at three-hit special bricks. The laser bypasses normal physics, making it the most efficient tool for high-health targets.
  3. Accept slower progress. These stages take longer. Rushing leads to sloppy paddle positioning and lost lives.

Expert Stages (11 – 20): Endurance and Focus

  1. Conserve lives aggressively. Every life lost is one you cannot get back unless an Extra Life drops. Play defensively, keeping the paddle under the ball at all times.
  2. Exploit multi-ball chains. If you already have two balls in play and collect another Multi-Ball, you jump to three. With three or more balls, the screen fills with projectiles and bricks crumble fast — but you must stay alert to avoid losing all of them at once.
  3. Use the walls. Side walls are your allies. A ball bouncing between a wall and a vertical stack of bricks clears the entire column without paddle intervention.

Advanced Ball Control

The difference between a casual player and a high scorer comes down to how deliberately they control the ball's trajectory. In Brick Breaker, the ball is not random — its direction after hitting the paddle is entirely determined by where the ball makes contact.

Paddle Hit Zones

The paddle is divided into zones. A hit at the dead center sends the ball nearly straight up with minimal horizontal velocity. A hit near the left or right edge adds significant horizontal velocity, sending the ball at a steep angle. Understanding this relationship lets you aim the ball with precision rather than reacting to wherever it goes.

Practice this technique: position the paddle so the ball hits slightly off-center toward the side where you want the ball to travel. Over time, you develop an intuitive feel for how far off-center produces the angle you need. This is the fundamental skill that separates players who reach stage 5 from those who reach stage 15.

The Ceiling Trap

The most powerful technique in Brick Breaker is getting the ball above the brick formation. When the ball is trapped between the ceiling and the top row of bricks, it bounces back and forth rapidly, destroying brick after brick without any paddle input. To trigger this, aim for a gap at the edge of the formation or wait until enough bricks are destroyed to create an opening in the top row. Once the ball is above, sit back and watch your score climb.

Speed Management

The ball's speed is capped at 1.5 times its base speed. Each paddle hit can add or subtract velocity based on the hit angle. If the ball is moving too fast for comfort, try hitting it near the center of the paddle. Center hits minimize the velocity change and keep the ball at a manageable speed. Catching a Slow Ball power-up at the right moment can turn a chaotic situation into a controlled one.

Multi-Ball Awareness

When multiple balls are in play, your eyes naturally want to track all of them. Resist this instinct. Instead, focus on the ball closest to the bottom of the screen — that is the one most likely to be lost. The other balls will take care of themselves by bouncing off walls and bricks. Only shift your focus when a different ball drops lower.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you play Brick Breaker?

Move the paddle left and right using your mouse, touch screen, or arrow keys to bounce a ball into rows of bricks. Each brick you hit is damaged or destroyed, earning points. Clear all breakable bricks on a stage to advance to the next level. You start with three lives and lose one each time the ball falls past the paddle. Collect falling power-ups for advantages like an expanded paddle, multi-ball, or extra lives.

What are the power-ups in Brick Breaker?

There are five power-ups: Paddle Expand widens your paddle for 10 seconds, Slow Ball reduces speed by 20% for 8 seconds, Multi-Ball splits your ball into two, Laser lets you shoot bricks directly, and Extra Life adds one life up to a maximum of five. Each power-up also awards 50 bonus points. They drop randomly with a 15% chance when any brick is destroyed.

How many levels are in Brick Breaker?

There are 20 stages in total. Stages 1 through 4 feature only normal bricks. Stages 5 through 7 introduce strong two-hit bricks. Stages 8 through 10 add special three-hit bricks and unbreakable steel bricks. Stages 11 through 20 combine all brick types in increasingly difficult patterns.

What is the best Brick Breaker strategy?

The most effective approach is to get the ball above the brick formation. Once trapped between the ceiling and the top row, the ball clears large sections automatically. Use deliberate paddle edge hits to aim for gaps in the formation. Prioritize Multi-Ball and Paddle Expand power-ups for the biggest advantage. In later stages, play defensively and conserve lives.

Is Brick Breaker the same as Arkanoid?

Both games share the same core ball-and-paddle mechanic originating from Atari's 1976 Breakout. Arkanoid, released by Taito in 1986, added power-ups, varied brick types, and boss encounters. DopaBrain's Brick Breaker follows the Arkanoid tradition with five power-up types, four brick varieties including unbreakable steel, 20 stages, neon visuals, particle effects, and smooth 60fps browser gameplay.
