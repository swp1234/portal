kg to pounds: Double the number and add 10%. 80 kg ≈ 160 + 16 = 176 lb (actual: 176.4 lb).

Celsius to Fahrenheit: Double the Celsius value and add 30. 20°C ≈ 40 + 30 = 70°F (actual: 68°F).

Metric vs Imperial: Understanding the Two Systems

One of the most common sources of confusion in unit conversion is the split between the metric system (also called SI, the International System of Units) and the imperial system (sometimes called the US customary system). Here is what you need to know:

The Metric System

Used by the vast majority of the world — roughly 95% of the global population — the metric system is built on base-10 relationships. One kilometer is exactly 1,000 meters. One kilogram is exactly 1,000 grams. One liter is exactly 1,000 milliliters. This decimal structure makes calculations straightforward and reduces errors, which is why it is the standard in science, medicine, and international trade.

The Imperial System

Primarily used in the United States, the imperial system has historical roots in British measurement standards. Its conversion factors are irregular: 1 mile = 5,280 feet, 1 pound = 16 ounces, 1 gallon = 4 quarts = 8 pints. While these relationships seem arbitrary compared to the metric system, they are deeply embedded in American culture, construction standards, and everyday language.

Why Both Still Exist

Despite decades of metrication efforts, the United States has never fully adopted the metric system for everyday use. American road signs, grocery labels, weather forecasts, and building codes remain imperial. Meanwhile, science, the military, and most international-facing industries use metric. This dual reality means Americans — and anyone interacting with American products, recipes, or measurements — need to convert between systems regularly. Tips for Accuracy

Getting the right number from a unit converter is easy. Getting the right answer for your specific situation requires a bit more thought. Here are practical tips to ensure your conversions are accurate and useful:

  • Know your precision needs. Cooking a recipe? Rounding to one decimal place is fine. Machining a metal part? You may need four or more decimal places. The converter provides high-precision results — choose the level of rounding appropriate to your task.
  • Watch out for US vs UK gallons. A US gallon is 3.785 liters, while an imperial (UK) gallon is 4.546 liters — a 20% difference. Always confirm which gallon a recipe or fuel economy figure is using.
  • Temperature is not linear. Unlike length or weight conversions (which are simple multiplication), temperature conversion between Celsius and Fahrenheit involves both multiplication and addition. A 10-degree increase in Celsius is an 18-degree increase in Fahrenheit, not a fixed ratio.
  • Double-check unit abbreviations. "oz" can mean fluid ounce (volume) or ounce (weight). "m" means meter, not mile. "mm" means millimeter, not million. Small misreadings lead to big errors.
  • Use the history feature. If you are doing multiple related conversions (like converting an entire recipe), the converter's history log helps you keep track of all your results without having to write them down separately.

What is the difference between metric and imperial?

The metric system uses base-10 relationships (1 km = 1000 m, 1 kg = 1000 g) and is used by most of the world. The imperial system uses irregular conversion factors (1 mile = 5,280 feet, 1 pound = 16 ounces) and is primarily used in the United States. The metric system is simpler for calculations, while imperial units persist in American daily life, construction, and cooking.

Can I convert temperature?

Yes. The converter supports Celsius, Fahrenheit, and Kelvin. Temperature conversion is unique because it uses both multiplication and addition: °F = (°C × 9/5) + 32, and °C = (°F − 32) × 5/9.
