What Is the MBTI Coffee Test?

The MBTI Coffee Test is a personality quiz that combines the Myers-Briggs Type Indicator framework with coffee culture. Instead of abstract personality questions, it places you in 8 realistic cafe scenarios: how you order, what kind of cafe atmosphere you prefer, what matters most in your coffee, your morning routine, who you bring along, how you react to a new menu item, whether you give honest feedback, and how you discover cafes while traveling.

Each response reveals clues about your four MBTI dimensions — Extraversion vs. Introversion, Sensing vs. Intuition, Thinking vs. Feeling, and Judging vs. Perceiving — and maps you to one of 16 coffee drinks that embodies your personality profile.

The concept draws on consumer psychology research showing that beverage preferences correlate with personality traits. A 2022 study published in the journal Appetite found that individuals with higher openness to experience were more likely to try unusual flavor combinations, while those scoring high in conscientiousness preferred familiar, reliable choices. The MBTI Coffee Test translates these academic findings into an engaging, shareable format.

The Psychology Behind Coffee Preferences

Why does your personality influence what you drink? The answer lies in how our cognitive preferences shape sensory experiences, social rituals, and decision-making patterns.

The Four Dimensions in Coffee Choices

E vs. I (Extraversion/Introversion): Extraverts often treat coffee as a social experience — meeting friends at trendy cafes, ordering elaborate drinks that spark conversation. Introverts tend to value coffee as personal ritual — a quiet morning moment with a carefully prepared cup.

S vs. N (Sensing/Intuition): Sensors appreciate the tangible qualities of coffee — exact temperature, specific roast profiles, consistent taste. Intuitives are drawn to the concept and story behind a drink — single-origin beans, experimental brewing methods, unusual flavor profiles.

T vs. F (Thinking/Feeling): Thinkers evaluate coffee objectively — caffeine content, cost-effectiveness, brewing efficiency. Feelers prioritize how coffee makes them feel — the warmth, the comfort, the sensory pleasure of a beautifully crafted latte.

J vs. P (Judging/Perceiving): Judgers have a set coffee routine — same order, same time, same cafe. Perceivers keep their options open — trying whatever catches their eye, exploring new cafes, adapting their order to their mood.

Analyst Types: NT Coffee Matches

The Analyst temperament — INTJ, INTP, ENTJ, ENTP — combines Intuition with Thinking. These types value efficiency, intellectual depth, and strategic thinking, which is reflected in coffee drinks that are bold, complex, or cleverly engineered.

INTJ — EspressoPure, concentrated, no nonsense. Like the Architect personality, Espresso wastes nothing. It delivers maximum intensity in minimum volume — strategic, efficient, and deeply respected by those who understand it.
INTP — Turkish CoffeeAncient, ritualistic, intellectually fascinating. Turkish Coffee requires patience and precision — much like the Logician's approach to problems. Its complex, unfiltered character mirrors the INTP's layered inner world.
ENTJ — Cold BrewMethodical preparation, powerful results. Cold Brew takes 12-24 hours of patient extraction to achieve its smooth, commanding flavor — embodying the Commander's long-term strategic thinking and natural authority.
ENTP — Double-Shot Flat WhiteBold innovation meets refined execution. The Double-Shot Flat White breaks convention by amplifying intensity while maintaining velvety sophistication — just like the Debater who challenges the status quo with charm.

Diplomat Types: NF Coffee Matches

Diplomats — INFJ, INFP, ENFJ, ENFP — combine Intuition with Feeling. They seek meaning, connection, and authenticity, gravitating toward coffee drinks with soul, story, and emotional depth.

INFJ — Chai LatteWarm, spiced, deeply comforting. Chai Latte blends complexity with nurturing warmth — like the Advocate who sees beneath the surface and offers quiet wisdom. Its aromatic layers mirror the INFJ's rich inner life.
INFP — Lavender LatteDreamy, unique, gently unconventional. Lavender Latte exists in its own category — neither traditional coffee nor simple flavor drink. Like the Mediator, it refuses to fit neatly into boxes and creates beauty through quiet rebellion.
ENFJ — MochaThe perfect balance of strength and sweetness. Mocha combines robust coffee with rich chocolate, creating something that brings people together. Like the Protagonist, it is generous, crowd-pleasing, and genuinely warm.
ENFP — Caramel MacchiatoSweet, layered, full of surprises. Caramel Macchiato delivers unexpected flavor transitions with every sip — embodying the Campaigner's enthusiastic, multilayered personality and infectious joy.

Sentinel Types: SJ Coffee Matches

Sentinels — ISTJ, ISFJ, ESTJ, ESFJ — combine Sensing with Judging. They value reliability, tradition, and structure, preferring coffee drinks that are consistent, well-crafted, and time-tested.

ISTJ — AmericanoReliable, straightforward, no-frills excellence. Americano is the backbone of any cafe — clean, consistent, and always delivering exactly what you expect. Like the Logistician, it earns trust through unwavering dependability.
ISFJ — Vanilla LatteComforting, familiar, quietly perfect. Vanilla Latte wraps you in warmth without demanding attention — like the Defender who shows love through small, consistent acts of care. It is the drink equivalent of a warm blanket.
ESTJ — Drip CoffeeEfficient, traditional, gets the job done. Drip Coffee is the executive's choice — brewed in bulk, served without ceremony, valued for its function. Like the Executive, it prioritizes results over style.
ESFJ — CappuccinoClassic, sociable, universally loved. Cappuccino is the cafe's social butterfly — familiar enough for everyone, elegant enough for any occasion. Like the Consul, it thrives in bringing people together over something warm and beautiful.

Explorer Types: SP Coffee Matches

Explorers — ISTP, ISFP, ESTP, ESFP — combine Sensing with Perceiving. They live in the present moment, seeking sensory experiences, freedom, and spontaneity in their coffee choices.

ISTP — Long BlackMinimalist, bold, quietly intense. Long Black strips away everything unnecessary to reveal pure coffee character. Like the Virtuoso, it is understated, independent, and deeply competent without needing to prove anything.
ISFP — Matcha LatteArtistic, natural, subtly vibrant. Matcha Latte is visually stunning and distinctly individual — it does not try to be coffee at all. Like the Adventurer, it follows its own aesthetic path with quiet confidence.
ESTP — Iced AmericanoCool, refreshing, always ready for action. Iced Americano is the grab-and-go choice for someone who does not slow down. Like the Entrepreneur, it is energetic, practical, and thrives in the fast lane.
ESFP — FrappuccinoFun, photogenic, unapologetically extra. Frappuccino is a celebration in a cup — whipped cream, drizzle, the works. Like the Entertainer, it turns an ordinary moment into an experience worth sharing.

How the Test Works

8 Cafe Scenario Questions

Rather than asking direct personality questions, the MBTI Coffee Test immerses you in real cafe situations. Each scenario has four options that subtly measure your MBTI preferences:

  • Ordering Style: Do you decide instantly, study the menu, ask the barista for recommendations, or try whatever is new?
  • Cafe Vibe: Do you want a quiet reading nook, a lively social space, a minimalist design cafe, or a cozy neighborhood spot?
  • Coffee Criteria: Is taste, caffeine strength, aesthetic presentation, or ethical sourcing most important to you?
  • Morning Routine: Same coffee same time, or do you improvise based on mood?
  • Companions: Solo coffee time, with one close friend, in a group, or with a partner?
  • New Menu Reaction: Try it immediately, research reviews first, stick to your usual, or wait for someone else to order it?
  • Honest Feedback: Would you tell a barista their new recipe needs work, or keep your thoughts to yourself?
  • Travel Discovery: Do you seek out famous cafes, follow local recommendations, wander until you find one, or stick to chains?
